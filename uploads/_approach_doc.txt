[Heading2] Editorial conventions in force across both pages
[ListParagraph] No em dashes. Periods, commas, or colons only. This rule is absolute.
[ListParagraph] No use of the abstract word holds in either copy or microcopy. Concrete substitutes: sustains, scales, continues, transfers, does not degrade. The verb hold appears only in idiomatic uses such as we hold ourselves to a different one.
[ListParagraph] No use of the soft-register word culture in copy. POWERS sells in verbs, not nouns. Behaviors, processes, systems, and operating discipline can be observed, measured, and improved. Culture cannot be tied to a P&amp;L line item, which is exactly why the repositioning moved away from Culture Powers Business as a tagline. Substitutes by context: organization, enterprise, operating environment, workplace, how the operation runs. The word behavior is fine and stays, behavior is observable, measurable, and POWERS improves it directly. Culture is the abstraction behavior gets confused with.
[ListParagraph] Use sites, not facilities. POWERS speaks about sites internally and with clients. Site is the unit of operational scope, a single-site Discovery, a multi-site engagement, performance that transfers across sites. Facility appears in the existing brochures but is being retired across the new site copy.
[ListParagraph] No use of the possessive Our in nav labels or page titles. The page is Approach, not Our Approach. Discovery Process, not Our Discovery Process.
[ListParagraph] Sentence case throughout headlines and subheads. Title case is reserved for proper nouns and program names such as Discovery, Implementation, Evaluate ROI &amp; Savings, Management Operating System, Project Savings Commitment, Key Event Schedule.
[ListParagraph] Apply text-wrap: balance to every H1, H2, and H3. Apply text-wrap: pretty to every body paragraph. Watch every section&apos;s closing line for orphans and widows. The widow watch notes embedded in this document call out the highest-risk lines.
